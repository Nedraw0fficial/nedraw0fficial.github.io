HALL OF FAME

Some space missions just hit different, you know? Like Sputnik being the first thing we ever put in orbit, or Apollo 11 landing on the Moon. Each one was basically us unlocking a new area on the map. We went from launching tin cans with beeps to driving robots around on Mars.

THE APOLLO LEGACY

Apollo was seriously insane. From 1969 to 1972, twelve people actually walked on the Moon. They grabbed rocks, ran experiments, planted flags - the whole deal. And it took thousands of people working together to pull it off, which is pretty crazy when you think about it.

A lot of the tech we invented for Apollo is still used today. The computer systems, the way we communicate with spacecraft, life support stuff - we basically figured out the basics back then and have been building on top of it ever since.

NEXT LEVEL: ARTEMIS

NASA's working on Artemis now, which is basically the next Moon mission. They want to send people back there and eventually use it as a stepping stone to Mars. So yeah, we're not done with space yet - not even close baby, TECHNOBLADE NEVER DIES!
